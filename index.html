Welcome

My love of fantasy and fiction began in the fifth grade with a furry-toed Halfling named Bilbo Baggins. Bilbo’s travels took him There and Back Again. I went with him, but never came back. My next summers were filled with Frodo’s adventures, as my siblings and I fought over installments of The Lord of the Rings. From there, reading transported me to other worlds, those of the Bronte sisters, Jane Austen, Dickens, and Dumas.

The birth of my own children brought with it the opportunity to pass a love of books down to the next generation. Together, we have thrilled at the perils of Harry Potter and the good creatures of Redwall Abbey. I snooped through the books they selected from the library, nabbing them for a quick read as one caught my fancy. Don’t expect to find me browsing titles in the “grown-up” section of a bookstore either. You’ll most likely find me in the Young Reader and Young Adult sections, scouting for finds among the stacks like a Niffler sniffing out bright, shiny objects.
